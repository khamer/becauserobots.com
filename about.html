When I work

My name is Kevin Hamer and I am a lead web engineer at iMarc in Newburyport. iMarc was founded in 1997 and has added a new wing and a second office while I've been there. It's a great atmosphere, and we like to really think things through, making sure every component of our projects have the same vision, goals, and direction.

I have a nice Lenovo X1 Carbon I use. I run GVIM and XFCE mostly, though I try to switch over and test out other things like Sublime Text's vintage mode, Gnome 3, and Unity occasionally. I prefer mercurial, but iMarc uses Subversion and Git seems unavoidable.

Besides the client projects I work on over there, my biggest open source project is Boilerplate, our alternative to using Twitter Bootstrap or such.

When I play

When I'm not being productive, I am often in /r/mechanicalkeyboards. I own four mechanical and three unusual form factor keyboards at the moment:

  • two Leopold FC-200RL/AB Tenkeyless keyboards with mechanical Cherry MX Black switches,
  • an IBM Model M2 Keyboard from 1993,
  • a Silicon Graphics keyboard that, unfortunately, won't work with modern computers,
  • a HHKB Lite 2 with rubber dome switches, and
  • both the Typematrix 2020 and Typematrix 2030 keyboards, although the 2020 doesn't work properly.

These days I've also become distracted with DayZ with a group known as Turkey Bravo (TKB), a zombie survival game. I'm also prone to spending time playing Kerbal Space Program, an exceptional indie game.

I still occasionally playing Minecraft as well, but between avoiding zombies and working my way to Jop, I keep myself entertained.
